# Regulatory Map: India

## Status: Emerging Framework (2026)

> India's regulatory framework for RWA tokenization is actively evolving. The **Asset Tokenisation Bill 2026**, introduced by MP Raghav Chadha, proposes a formal legal framework for tokenized real-world assets. Monitor legislative progress closely.

## Current Framework (Pre-Bill)

| Area | Regulator | Current Position |
|------|-----------|------------------|
| Securities | SEBI | Tokenized securities = securities; SEBI oversight applies |
| Payments | RBI | Stablecoins / e-money tokens under RBI scrutiny |
| Crypto assets | FEMA / IT Act | No specific crypto framework yet |
| AML | FIU-IND | PMLA obligations apply to VASPs |

## Asset Tokenisation Bill 2026 (Proposed)

- Introduced by MP Raghav Chadha in Parliament
- Proposes legal recognition of tokenized RWAs
- Expected to cover: land records, commodities, infrastructure assets
- Regulatory oversight: likely SEBI (securities) + RBI (monetary assets)
- Status: Under parliamentary review — **monitor for updates**

## Practical Guidance (Current)

- Tokenized equity / debt instruments = SEBI-regulated securities
- Regulatory clarity for real estate, commodities tokenization = limited
- Recommend obtaining legal opinion from India-qualified counsel before launching
- AML/KYC: PMLA obligations apply; FIU-IND reporting as required
- Foreign investor participation: FEMA approval may be required

## ERC-3643 Notes

- On-chain compliance module approach aligns with likely SEBI direction for regulated tokenized securities
- Investor registry (ONCHAINID) maps well to SEBI demat account concepts
- Watch for SEBI consultation papers on tokenized securities — likely to follow Bill progress
